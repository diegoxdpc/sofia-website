Biography

Sofía Margarita Vergara Vergara, born on July 10, 1972, in Barranquilla, Colombia, is one of the most influential Latina actresses and entrepreneurs in the global entertainment industry. Affectionately known as "Toti," she began her career after being discovered by a photographer on a Colombian beach, leading to an iconic Pepsi commercial and hosting work on Fuera de Serie.

Her definitive leap to international stardom came with her role as Gloria Delgado-Pritchett in Modern Family (2009–2020), earning Emmy and Golden Globe nominations and becoming the highest-paid actress on American television for several years.

Career & Business

Beyond comedy, she demonstrated her versatility in the Netflix miniseries Griselda (2024), serving as executive producer and lead actress.

She is a judge on America's Got Talent and leads Latin World Entertainment. She owns her coffee brand ¡DÍOS MIO! and collaborates in fashion and beauty sectors.

After overcoming thyroid cancer in 2000, she created foundations supporting children and families affected by the disease in Colombia.

Family & Personal Life

Her only son, Manolo González-Ripoll Vergara (born 1991), is a director, producer, actor, and culinary entrepreneur.

Her parents were Margarita Vergara Dávila and Julio Enrique Vergara Robayo, a cattle rancher in Barranquilla.

She was married to Joe González-Ripoll (1991–1993) and Joe Manganiello (2015–2023). She has recently been linked to Douglas Chabbott.

Her siblings include Rafael (tragically murdered in 1998), Julio (faced addiction struggles), Verónica, Sandra Vergara (cousin adopted as sister), and Claudia Vergara (niece).
